PPP Dog Certification Guide

Understanding Potentially Dangerous Dogs Regulations in Barcelona

Official Reference: BOE-A-2002-6016

Pit Bull Terrier

  • • Strong and muscular build
  • • Requires PPP license
  • • Muzzle mandatory in public

Staffordshire Bull Terrier

  • • Muscular and agile
  • • PPP certification needed
  • • Special training required

Rottweiler

  • • Large and powerful
  • • PPP license mandatory
  • • Strict control in public

Dogo Argentino

  • • Athletic and strong
  • • PPP requirements apply
  • • Special handling needed

Fila Brasileiro

  • • Large and powerful
  • • PPP certification required
  • • Experienced owner needed

Tosa Inu

  • • Japanese mastiff breed
  • • PPP license needed
  • • Special regulations apply

Akita Inu

  • • Large Japanese breed
  • • PPP certification required
  • • Special handling needed

Crossbreeds

  • • Mixed with PPP breeds
  • • Similar characteristics to PPP
  • • License may be required

Key Requirements

Owner Requirements

  • Must be 18 or older
  • Clean criminal record
  • Physical & psychological fitness certificate
  • Civil liability insurance (€150,253 minimum)

Walking Requirements

  • Non-extendible lead (max 2 meters)
  • Muzzle required
  • One dog per person only

Important Notes

  • • License is valid for 5 years throughout Spain
  • • Annual insurance renewal proof required
  • • Fines range from €60 to €30,000 for non-compliance

1. Confirm PPP Breed Status

  • Check if your dog belongs to regulated breeds:
    Pit Bull Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, Dogo Argentino, Fila Brasileiro, Tosa Inu, Akita Inu, and crossbreeds with aggressive traits
  • Official reference: Barcelona PPP Regulations

2. Prepare Required Documents

Owner Documents:

  • • Valid ID (Passport/NIE)
  • • Padrón registration
  • • Criminal record certificate
  • • Psychological fitness certificate

Dog Documents:

  • • Microchip registration
  • • Rabies vaccination proof
  • • Health certificate
  • • Liability insurance (€120k+)

3. Submit Application

  1. 1
    Online Appointment:
    Book appointment via Barcelona City Hall
  2. 2
    In-Person Submission:
    Visit Citizens' Office with all documents
    Fee: €30-50 (subject to change)

4. After Submission

Processing time: ~15 working days
License validity: 5 years

Public Rules:

  • Always use muzzle and ≤2m leash
  • Maximum one PPP dog per handler
  • Prohibited in children's play areas
